A city in East Java Province, Indonesia. The city is located 130 km southwest of Surabaya and is the third largest city in East Java after Surabaya and Malang by population. The city of Kediri has an area of 63.40 km¬≤ and the entire territory is bordered by Kediri Regency. The city of Kediri is divided by the Brantas River which stretches from south to north for 7 kilometers. The population of this city is 292,363 people, based on the Ministry of Home Affairs registration data for the city of Kediri in 2021

Kediri is known as a major trading center for sugar and the largest cigarette industry in Indonesia. In this city, too, the Gudang Garam clove cigarette factory was established and growing. In 2010, Kediri was named the first rank in Indonesia, namely Most Recommended City for Investment
